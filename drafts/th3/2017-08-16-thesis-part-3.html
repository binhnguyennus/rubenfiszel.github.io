[thesis III] An interpreter for spatial and a spatial implementation of a Rao-Blackwellized Particle Filter

Posted on August 16, 2017 by Ruben Fiszel

About

This post is the part III out of III of my master thesis at the DAWN lab, Stanford, under Prof. Kunle and Prof. Odersky supervision. The central themes of this thesis are sensor fusion and spatial, an hardware accelerator language (Verilog is also one, but tedious).

This part is about the spatial language, the development of its interpreter and the spatial implementation of the filter presented in part I.
